most Arabic words are derived from a 3, 4, or 5 letter root, which typically comprise a past tense verb.

رسم (drew), كتب (wrote), صنع (created)

words take similar 'scales', or templates, such as by having the same prefixes or letter inserts to give parallel meanings.

راسم (drawer), كاتب (writer), صانع (creator)

the three-letter root ف-ع-ل (f-a'-l), referring to 'doing', and all its variations, are often thought of as a blueprint, the baseline for thinking of scales.

on the left, you'll see the ف-ع-ل scale, and on the right the corresponding word for a different three-letter root.
